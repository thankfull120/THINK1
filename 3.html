PSALM

Hymns[edit] Hymns, songs of praise for God's work in creation or history.
They typically open with a call to praise, describe the motivation for praise, and conclude with a repetition of the call.

Two sub-categories are "enthronement psalms", celebrating the enthronement of Yahweh as king,

and Zion psalms, glorifying Mount Zion, God's dwelling-place in Jerusalem.[9]

Gunkel also described a special subset of "eschatological hymns"[10]

which includes themes of future restoration (Psalm 126) or of judgment (Psalm 82).[11]
